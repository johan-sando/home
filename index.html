About

Howdy! I am an undergraduate researcher majoring in Biomedical Engineering with a minor in mathematics that is interested in the integration of biological ideas into the world of robotic control. My main research interest lies in translating and applying mathematical frameworks derived from biomechanics to control systems for biomimetic, legged, and exoskeleton systems.

Currently, I am working in the HERC Lab as an undergraduate researcher. Last semester's project was centered around creating a custom piece of testing hardware for an upcoming paper. Said project allowed me to hone my skills in ergonomic design as well as the complexities of interacting with CAD and additive manufacturers such as OSH Cut. This upcoming semester, I expect to be able to learn more about Kalman Filters and their applications to knee exoskeletons with the possibility of a paper being published.

Since, roughly, sophomore year I have worked as a part-time tutor at Blinn College. I have been responsible for tutoring a vast array of subjects ranging from electromagnetism to differential equations to organic chemistry. I have developed a solid communication style that lends itself to translating complex topics into a more understandable framework based on a tutee's proficiency in the subject.

Outside of academic and professional work, you can usually find me swimming laps, sketching, or playing video games with my family.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities in mechanical engineering and robotics for the Fall 2027 cycle, with a particular interest in labs that are working on in biomimetic design, wearable robotics, and embodied AI.
